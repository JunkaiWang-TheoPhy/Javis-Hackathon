## Current Blockers

Latest observed state:

- `health_connect_ready = false`
- `bluetooth_ready = true`
- `health_connect_status = xiaomi_provider_incompatible_interface`
- `band_status = bonded`
- `android.permission.BLUETOOTH_CONNECT = granted=true`
- `android.permission.POST_NOTIFICATIONS = granted=true`
- the phone still does not expose the official `com.google.android.apps.healthdata` provider package

This means the transport, Bluetooth status polling, and desktop bridge are working. The current blocker is now narrower:

- HyperOS exposes Xiaomi Fitness health pages and a Xiaomi-specific health service
- that Xiaomi service does not implement the binder interface expected by Jetpack `androidx.health.connect:connect-client`
- so heart rate, SpO2, and steps remain `null` on the current code path even though the band is bonded and the gateway is live

The gateway app now routes `Open Health Connect` and `Grant Health Permissions` to Xiaomi Fitness fallback pages on this phone, but those pages alone will not make the Jetpack health reader become readable unless a compatible Google Health Connect provider is also installed.

## Manual Steps Still Required On The Phone

1. Keep `Mi Band Gateway` in foreground when starting the gateway.
2. If Play Store is available, install or update official `Health Connect` (`com.google.android.apps.healthdata`).
3. In Xiaomi Fitness, allow sync/write access into Health Connect if that option appears.
4. In `Mi Band Gateway`, tap:
   - `Grant Android Permissions`
   - `Open Health Connect`
   - `Grant Health Permissions`
   - `Start Gateway`

## Expected Next-State Signal

If a compatible Google Health Connect provider becomes available and Xiaomi Fitness writes into it, these values should change:

- `health_connect_ready` should become `true`
- `bluetooth_ready` should become `true`
- `band_status` should remain `bonded` or move to `connected`
- `/health/latest` should begin returning non-null `heart_rate_bpm`, `spo2_percent`, and `steps`
